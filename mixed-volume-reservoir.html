These are not the full concept entries. They are previews of the objects that will later be developed in the dedicated concept shelf page.

Preview

Newton polytopes

A polynomial support becomes a finite set of exponent vectors. Its convex hull is the Newton polytope, turning algebraic sparsity into geometry.

supports convex hulls
Preview

Mixed volume

Mixed volume is the multilinear coefficient hidden in the volume of a Minkowski combination. It measures how several polytopes interact.

Minkowski sums BKK bound
Preview

Bernstein’s theorem

For a generic sparse system, the number of isolated solutions in the algebraic torus is controlled by the mixed volume of the Newton polytopes.

sparse systems torus solutions

The full concept shelf should explain these entries slowly, with examples, diagrams, and links to the papers where they become operational.
