Mathematics · Athens

Exploring the architecture of mathematics.

I am Constantinos Gkogkotsis, an undergraduate student in the Department of Mathematics at the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens.

My interests are centered around algebra, geometry, and logic, together with the computational methods through which theoretical structures can become explicit.

Mathematical direction

My present center of gravity is the interaction between algebraic, geometric, logical, and computational methods. I am interested in mathematics both as abstract structure and as something that can be made explicit through calculations, algorithms, and examples.

Primary axis

  • Algebra
  • Geometry
  • Logic
  • Structural mathematics

Computational axis

  • Algorithms
  • Computational algebra
  • Graph-theoretic methods
  • Explicit calculation

Developing interests

  • Polynomial systems
  • Gröbner bases
  • Mixed-volume methods
  • Connections between theory and computation
