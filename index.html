Mathematics · Athens

Exploring the architecture of mathematics.

I am Constantinos Gkogkotsis, an undergraduate student in the Department of Mathematics at the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens.

My current aim is to develop a coherent foundation in theoretical mathematics, with increasing attention to research-level questions, methods, and exposition.

My main orientation is toward algebra, geometry, and logic, and the structural problems that arise between them.

Mathematical direction

My mathematical direction is still being formed, but its main axis is theoretical: algebra, geometry, and logic. I am interested in the way these areas intersect and formulate problems. Computation enters as a secondary direction, mainly when it helps make abstract phenomena explicit.

Primary axis

  • Algebra
  • Geometry
  • Logic

Computational axis

  • Analysis of Algorithms
  • Computational algebra
  • Computability Theory

Developing interests

  • Sparse Polynomial Systems and Mixed Volumes
  • Quantitative Semantics of Lambda Calculus
  • Operator Theory and Operator Algebras
